Why Regular Wheel Alignment Matters

Wheel alignment is more than just keeping your car straight. It affects safety, performance, and cost savings. Here’s why you should take it seriously:

1. Better Fuel Efficiency

Misaligned wheels make your car work harder, burning more fuel.

2. Longer Tyre Life

Uneven alignment causes tyres to wear out faster and unevenly.

3. Improved Handling

A properly aligned car handles smoothly, keeping you in better control.

4. Safety on the Road

Misalignment increases braking distance and can cause drifting — dangerous at high speeds.

5. Saves Money Long Term

Proper alignment prevents premature tyre replacements and reduces mechanical strain.

Final Tip: Get your alignment checked every 5,000 km or sooner if you hit potholes or curbs often.
